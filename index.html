MMA Training Plan — Final v4
Home + Gym split · Heavy bag · 6 days/week · 6 months · Calf-safe
Shopping list before Week 1: 16oz boxing gloves (~$30–50) + 180" hand wraps (~$10) + mouthguard (~$15). Total under $75. Don't buy: bag gloves (too thin, risk wrist injury) or MMA gloves (only for sparring).
Calf pain check first: If sharp, localised, or present at rest — see a physio before starting. Plan assumes general deconditioning tightness. No improvement in 2 weeks of daily stretching means get it assessed.
Every session: 5-min warm-up (joint circles → swings → light shadowboxing) + neck isometrics (4 directions × 10 sec) + calf stretch (30 sec each leg). Non-negotiable.

Home vs gym split
At home: bag work, shadowboxing, grappling movement drills, mobility, conditioning circuits. At gym: compound lifts (squat, deadlift, bench, row, overhead press) — everything your bodyweight plan can't fully replicate. This split means you never need MMA equipment at the gym — just the strength rack and basics.
🥊
Heavy bag protocol (leg-only bag)
Since your bag is heavy/weighted for legs (likely a floor-anchored kick bag), it's great for kicks and knees but too rigid for heavy punches — you'll hurt your wrists. For punches: use shadowboxing for precision and light taps on the bag only (50% power) for timing. For kicks: full power is fine and that's where your bag shines. Always wrap hands + wear gloves, even for light bag work.
🏋
Gym strength principles
Start light — form over weight for the first 3 weeks regardless of your old numbers. Core lifts: goblet squat → back squat, Romanian deadlift → conventional deadlift, bench press, barbell row, overhead press. Rep scheme: 3×8–10 in Phase 1, 4×6–8 in Phase 2–3. Rest: 2–3 min between heavy sets. Progress by 2.5kg when you hit all reps with good form.
N
Neck work — every training day
MMA is brutal on necks and yours has been inactive for a year. Do these before every session as part of warm-up. 3×10 sec isometric holds in 4 directions: hand on forehead push forward, hand behind head push back, hand on each temple push side. Plus 10 slow neck circles each direction. Takes 3 minutes. Never skip.
🦵
Calf return protocol
Jogging only after passing: walk 45 min pain-free → 15 single-leg calf raises on affected leg → zero next-day soreness. Then: 1 min jog / 2 min walk intervals. Jump rope banned first 4 weeks of running return. Kicks on the bag are fine earlier — they don't load the calf the way running does.
🥗
Recovery baseline
Protein: 1.6–2g per kg bodyweight daily (~100–140g for most). Sleep: 7–9 hrs — this is where adaptation happens. Hydration: ~35ml per kg bodyweight, more on training days. Carbs: don't cut them on training days. Gym work adds recovery demand — eat enough.
